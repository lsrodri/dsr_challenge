Non-linear learners: Random forests, gradient-boosted trees (XGBoost, CatBoost), or kernel methods can flexibly model interactions and thresholds in weather/dengue dynamics.

Time-aware cross-validation: Use a rolling-window or expanding-window split rather than random K-fold to better mimic forecasting on future weeks.

Rich feature engineering:

Lagged targets (last week’s cases, 2-week moving average)

Weather anomalies (departures from long-term monthly means)

Interaction terms (e.g. precipitation × humidity)

Hyperparameter search: A randomized or grid search for regularization strengths (alpha, l1_ratio) or tree-based parameters (max_depth, learning_rate) may yield additional gains.

Outlier strategy: Experiment with turning off outlier removal entirely, or using a milder threshold—some extreme weeks might carry important early-outbreak signals rather than noise.
